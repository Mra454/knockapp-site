What Knock is

Knock is a venue-based social app that helps people signal they're open to connection, at a coffee shop, library, or wherever you find yourself. Check in, see who else is there, send a knock, and chat if they accept.

Your privacy is the foundation of how we've designed this. You are anonymous by default. Any further details you share during a conversation are entirely your choice.

Information we collect

When you use Knock, we may collect:

  • Your nickname (not your real name, you choose this)
  • Your selected aura (a color/vibe preference)
  • Your anonymous account identifier (auto-generated, not tied to your identity)
  • The venue you check in to
  • Knocks you send or receive, and chat messages

We do not require your real name, email, or phone number to create or use Knock. If you contact support, we collect the information you choose to provide.

No manual sign-up is required. Knock creates a pseudonymous app account to support check-ins, messaging, and safety features.

Location data

Knock uses your location while the app is open to identify nearby places you can check in to. That's it.

  • We do not track your location in the background
  • We do not store your raw GPS coordinates as part of normal app flow
  • We do not track your movement over time
Knock uses Google Places to retrieve nearby venue names when you check in. Your device sends location data to Google for this purpose, governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

How we use your information

We use data only to make the app work:

  • Let you check in and appear to others at the same venue
  • Show you signals checked in to the same place
  • Deliver knocks and messages between matched users
  • Support moderation and safety features
  • Keep the app functioning and improve reliability

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or any purpose outside of operating the app.

Authentication and backend services

Knock uses Supabase for anonymous authentication, data storage, and real-time messaging. Supabase may process your anonymous user ID, nickname, aura, venue presence, knocks, and messages. Data is hosted on AWS infrastructure.

Data is transmitted over encrypted HTTPS. Authentication tokens are stored locally on your device. No passwords are required.

Messaging and moderation

Messages are stored so chats can function in real time. Knock uses a banned-word filter and may review flagged content to enforce community safety.

Data sharing

We do not sell your personal information.

We share limited data only with service providers that help us operate the app:

  • Supabase, authentication, backend data, and real-time messaging
  • Google Places, if enabled, for nearby venue lookup at check-in time

We do not share your data with advertisers, data brokers, or any third party for marketing.

What we don't do

  • Sell, rent, or share your data for marketing
  • Use Bluetooth or QR codes to identify or track you
  • Track your location in the background
  • Require a real name, email, or phone number
  • Show your profile outside of your current venue session
  • Use cross-app tracking or advertising SDKs

Your choices

  • Whether to check in to a venue at all
  • What nickname to display
  • Whether to accept or pass on any Knock
  • To leave a venue or end a chat at any time

You can request deletion directly from Settings inside the app. You can also email [email protected] and we will remove your account and all associated data promptly.

Data retention

Venue presence records are ephemeral and cleared when you leave or your session ends. Knock and message records are retained for the duration of the session and for up to 30 days afterward for safety review, then deleted.

Children's privacy

Knock is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 13. If you believe a child has provided information through the app, contact us and we will delete it promptly.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will update the effective date at the top of this page. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
